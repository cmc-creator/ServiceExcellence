# Audio Voiceover Pack

This pack provides short narration scripts, transition lines, and accessibility text for the annual training experience.

## Usage Notes

- Keep narration calm, steady, and warm.
- Leave a small pause before each prompt or transition.
- Use the same wording in captions and transcripts to avoid drift.

## Intro Narration

"Welcome to the Excellence Vault. In this annual psychiatric acute inpatient mission, every choice you make strengthens trust, safety, and integrity at Destiny Springs Healthcare."

"Select your role track, then work through the training path built for your daily workflow."

## Module Transition Narration

- Welcome to the first check-in. Focus on the behavior that protects trust, clarity, and safety.
- Service excellence is a repeatable sequence, not a slogan.
- Code of Conduct decisions protect people, colleagues, and the organization.
- Now the decision lab opens. Choose, reflect, and learn from the immediate feedback.
- You are in the final stretch. Speed matters, standards matter more.

## Response and Feedback Cues

- Correct answer cue: "Correct. That choice keeps the response aligned with policy and the people involved."
- Retry cue: "Not quite. Recheck the safest first action and the next escalation step."
- Completion cue: "Training complete. Review your score, acknowledge the annual attestation, and submit when ready."

## Ambient and Sound Guidance

- Ambient loop: subtle, low-volume room tone with no strong melody.
- Success cue: short, soft confirmation tone.
- Warning cue: short, low-volume alert tone that does not feel punitive.
- Badge cue: brief warm pulse tone.

## Caption and Transcript Text

Caption block example:

```text
[00:00] Welcome to the Excellence Vault.
[00:05] In this annual psychiatric acute inpatient mission, every choice you make strengthens trust, safety, and integrity at Destiny Springs Healthcare.
[00:14] Select your role track, then work through the training path built for your daily workflow.
```

Transcript note:

- Keep speaker labels simple.
- Preserve the same action wording used in the interactive cards.
- Include any on-screen warning or completion text verbatim when it appears.
