# Manager Toolkit

Use this guide after a learner completes the annual training to turn the score into a short coaching conversation.

## Purpose

- Reinforce one strong decision.
- Correct one repeat risk.
- Agree on one follow-up action before the next shift or workweek.

## 15-Minute Coaching Flow

1. Review the completion result and the learner's highest-risk gap.
2. Ask the learner to describe the first action they would take in that scenario again.
3. Identify one habit, communication step, or escalation step to practice next.
4. Capture a due date for the follow-up action and assign ownership.

## Discussion Questions

- What was the most realistic scenario in today's session?
- Which decision felt easiest, and which one would slow you down in real life?
- Where would you want a teammate to step in sooner?
- What phrase or action will you use the next time this situation appears?

## Role-Specific Coaching Focus

- Clinical: observation clarity, escalation timing, and immediate safety sequencing.
- Non-clinical: access control, message accuracy, and privacy-aware communication.
- Leadership: role assignment, follow-through, and visible policy enforcement.

## Suggested Follow-Up Actions

- Revisit the module tied to the learner's weakest category.
- Shadow one real workflow or drill step within the next 30 days.
- Document the coaching note in the manager's local tracking system.
